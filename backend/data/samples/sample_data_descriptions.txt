# Sample Data Descriptions

These CSV files represent different financial behaviors corresponding to the 5 credit score tiers.

## 1. Excellent Credit (excellent.csv)
- **Score Range**: 800-850
- **Characteristics**:
    - High average balance (> $40k).
    - Perfect payment consistency (Monthly salary like clockwork).
    - Long history (Data starts from previous year).
    - Low spending relative to income (High savings rate).

## 2. Very Good Credit (very_good.csv)
- **Score Range**: 740-799
- **Characteristics**:
    - Strong balance growth.
    - Evidence of investments (Financial responsibility).
    - No missed payments.
    - Stable income source.

## 3. Good Credit (good.csv)
- **Score Range**: 670-739
- **Characteristics**:
    - "Average" healthy profile.
    - Pays bills on time.
    - Moderate balance/savings.
    - No negative flags (overdrafts), but lower income/savings than top tiers.

## 4. Fair Credit (fair.csv)
- **Score Range**: 580-669
- **Characteristics**:
    - High spending volatility (Living paycheck to paycheck).
    - Low average balance.
    - Occasional high utilization (Spending almost all income).
    - **No missed payments**, but the financial stress lowers the score.

## 5. Poor Credit (poor.csv)
- **Score Range**: 300-579
- **Characteristics**:
    - **Missed Income**: Gap between Jan and March (Missed Feb).
    - **Overdrafts**: Balance frequently goes below zero.
    - Low/Negative balance.
